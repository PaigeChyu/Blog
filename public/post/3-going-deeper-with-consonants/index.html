(3) Going Deeper with Consonants

2020年1月23日
| Phonics

Key points

  • Gettig into Blending
  • Looking at consonants and keywords: p, r, n, b
  • Sight Words: one, red, yellow, boat

Materials

  • cards: p, r, n, b
  • sight word cards: one, red, yellow, boat
  • crayon

Sound of p, r

Instructor: The name of this letter is “pee”; the sound of this letter is p-p-p.

What is its name?

what is its sound?

show the p card with key word "pup"

Instructor: This keyword helps you to remember the letter p. Every time you see this letter, I want you to see the picture of a pup in your mind, and I want you to hear the sound of the first letter. This picture will unlock the sound of this letter just as a key unlocks the door.

let the child draw a "pup" on the card

Instructor: Say its sound, not its name.

Continue the script method with r.

Use the following keywords to help your child learn the p and r

Pp: pup
Rr: rat

Blending table for P and R

r p r p r p
ra pa ra pa ra Pa
ra t pa d ra g pa t ra p Pa m
rat pad rag pat rap Pam

After your child maters the words in the above table, have him read the following sentences for practice.

Pam had a map.

Dad had a fat ram.

A rat had a pad.

Sound of n, b

Use the following keywords to help your child learn the f and h

Nn: nap Bb: bat

Blending table for N and B

n b n b n b
na ba na ba na ba
na p ba t na g ba n na b ba d
nap bat nag ban nab bad

After your child maters the words in the above table, have him read the following sentences for practice.

Dan and Ann ran.

Mag and Pam nab Nat.

Tad and Dad nab Nat.
